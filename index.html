The approach

My content has a different goal than most.

I do not want someone to finish watching one of my films and scroll straight to the next thing. I want something to have shifted. Even slightly. A bit more understanding of the world. A bit more understanding of themselves.

The part of this work I care most about is making people feel genuinely at ease. My goal on any shoot is for the person in front of the camera to forget it is there. To feel like they are just having a conversation with someone they already know. That quality of presence cannot be faked and it is what separates footage that feels alive from footage that feels performed.

I am not interested in the dopamine hook formula. I want to make work that promotes real human excellence. Emotionally, creatively, spiritually. Work that helps people grow rather than keeps them stuck in a scroll.

Not performance. Not manipulation. Just truth, told with care.

About

It started with a simple belief: there were people whose stories deserved to be told properly, and no one had sat with them long enough to find out what those stories were.

That belief became a career. I'm now a full time filmmaker and director based in Glasgow, working across the UK, Dubai and Pakistan on documentary, sport and cultural work.

I shoot, direct and edit everything myself on a Sony A7IV. I work fast, I work lean, and I get inside the room in a way that is hard to manufacture from the outside.
